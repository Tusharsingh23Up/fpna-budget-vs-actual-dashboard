## Project Overview
This is an end-to-end Financial Analyst / FP&A portfolio project focused on budget vs actual variance analysis, profitability tracking, and revenue forecasting.

The project simulates how a finance team analyzes monthly business performance by comparing actual results against planned budgets. It helps identify where the company performed better or worse than expected and provides recommendations for improving future profitability.

## Business Problem
Management wants to understand why actual revenue, expenses, and profit differ from the approved budget. The goal is to identify key financial variances, explain performance gaps, forecast upcoming results, and recommend actions for better financial control.

## Project Objectives
- Analyze monthly budget vs actual performance
- Calculate revenue and expense variances
- Track profitability KPIs
- Identify favorable and unfavorable financial trends
- Forecast future revenue and profit
- Build a dashboard-ready dataset

## Key Insights
- Revenue performance improved during stronger business months.
- Expense growth in some months created unfavorable cost variance.
- Profit margin declined when expense growth exceeded revenue growth.
- Better budget monitoring can improve financial control.
- Rolling forecasts can help management make faster decisions.

## Recommendations
- Monitor budget variance every month.
- Investigate high-variance expense categories.
- Improve cost control in departments with recurring overspending.
- Reallocate spending toward high-return business areas.
- Use rolling forecasts instead of relying only on static annual budgets.
- Track revenue growth and profit margin together, not separately.
